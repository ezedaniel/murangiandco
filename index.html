SERVICES / FOR BUSINESSES
Financial Statements
& Statutory Filings.
Every deadline met. Every filing done right. Your business protected.
THE PROBLEM
Statutory filing is the part of running a business that most owners dread — and for good reason. The deadlines are multiple, the requirements overlap, and the consequences of getting it wrong are real. Companies House and HMRC operate on different filing calendars, and many business owners are unaware that their accounts filing deadline and their Corporation Tax deadline are not the same date — each carrying its own penalty regime.
Accounts prepared without proper accounting knowledge often contain errors — misclassified expenses, incorrect depreciation, missing disclosures — that can cause problems with HMRC or misrepresent the financial position of the business. VAT returns filed incorrectly can result in investigations, penalties, and unexpected bills. And when statutory accounts do not accurately reflect the business’s financial position, lenders, investors, and potential partners are making decisions based on wrong information.
Late filing penalties with Companies House start small but escalate quickly. HMRC late filing and payment penalties can become significant, particularly when interest compounds over time. With a qualified professional handling these obligations, it simply does not happen.
WHAT WE OFFER
Everything you need, filed on time.
Annual Financial Statements
We prepare your statutory financial statements in accordance with applicable accounting standards — for most small companies, that means FRS 102 Section 1A or FRS 105 for micro-entities. This includes the Profit and Loss Account, Balance Sheet, notes to the accounts where required, and the Directors’ report where applicable. Accounts are prepared from your underlying records, reviewed for accuracy and completeness, and formatted for both Companies House submission and your own management use.
Companies House Filings
We handle the preparation and submission of your annual accounts to Companies House, as well as your confirmation statement — the annual filing that confirms your company’s registered details are accurate and up to date. Both are submitted on time, every time.
Corporation Tax Return (CT600)
We prepare and submit your Corporation Tax return to HMRC, including computation of taxable profits, identification of all allowable deductions and reliefs, and calculation of the tax liability due. Any capital allowances your business is entitled to are correctly claimed.
VAT Returns
Where applicable, we prepare and submit your VAT returns on a quarterly or monthly basis, ensuring input and output tax is correctly calculated and that all returns are filed through MTD-compatible software in line with HMRC requirements.
Deadline Management
We track all relevant filing and payment deadlines on your behalf and communicate them to you well in advance — so nothing is ever a surprise, and nothing is ever late.

SERVICES / FOR BUSINESSES
Making Tax Digital
& Compliance.
The deadline is closer than you think. Get ahead of it now.
THE PROBLEM
Making Tax Digital is one of the most significant changes to the UK tax system in a generation — and the majority of small business owners are either unaware of what it requires, unclear on when it applies to them, or assuming it is someone else’s problem to solve.
The timeline is moving fast. MTD for Income Tax applies from April 2026 to sole traders and landlords with income above £50,000. The threshold drops to £30,000 in April 2027 and £20,000 in April 2028. Many people affected by the 2026 deadline do not yet know it applies to them.
Businesses using spreadsheets, manual records, or older accounting software do not meet HMRC’s MTD requirements and will need to change. Businesses already filing VAT returns digitally sometimes assume they are fully MTD compliant — they are not. MTD for Income Tax is a separate and more extensive requirement. And even businesses using Xero still need to be formally registered for MTD; compatibility and registration are not the same thing.
HMRC’s points-based penalty system means missed or late quarterly submissions will accumulate penalty points, eventually triggering financial penalties. The businesses that manage this transition smoothly are the ones that act before the deadline — not at it.
WHAT WE OFFER
Everything you need, end-to-end.
MTD Readiness Assessment
We start with a clear assessment of where your business currently stands in relation to MTD requirements — what applies to you, when, and what needs to change. This removes the confusion and gives you a straightforward picture of what needs to happen.
Digital Records Setup
If your business is not yet maintaining digital records in an MTD-compatible format, we set that up through Xero, which is fully compatible with HMRC’s requirements. Your records will be structured, categorised, and maintained in a way that satisfies MTD obligations from day one.
HMRC Registration
We handle your formal MTD registration with HMRC, ensuring your business is correctly enrolled and that your software is properly linked to your tax account. This is a step many businesses miss — and a necessary one.
Quarterly Submission Management
Under MTD for Income Tax, businesses are required to submit quarterly updates to HMRC summarising income and expenses. We manage these submissions on your behalf — accurately, on time, and through compliant software — so the quarterly obligation becomes a routine part of your financial calendar rather than a source of stress.
End of Period Statement & Final Declaration
At the end of each tax year, MTD requires an End of Period Statement and a Final Declaration to be submitted to HMRC. We prepare and submit both, incorporating any year-end adjustments and ensuring your tax position is accurately reported.
Ongoing Compliance Monitoring
MTD requirements are still evolving. We monitor developments and ensure your business remains compliant as the rules are refined — so you are never caught out by a change you were not aware of.

SERVICES / FOR INDIVIDUALS
Personal Tax Returns
& Planning.
The right return, on time. A plan that stops you overpaying.
THE PROBLEM
Self Assessment is the system HMRC uses to collect tax from individuals whose tax is not fully handled through PAYE. Freelancers, sole traders, landlords, higher earners, and individuals with investment income are all required to file — and many people are surprised to learn that even a small amount of additional income can trigger the obligation. The penalties for getting it wrong are not trivial: £100 for being a day late, daily penalties after three months, and interest accruing on any tax due.
Filing the return is only half the picture. Understanding how to legally and legitimately reduce your tax liability is the other — and most people are simply unaware of what is available to them, either missing reliefs they were entitled to claim or paying more tax than they need to. Tax planning simply means structuring your affairs to make full use of the reliefs and allowances that Parliament has deliberately put in place. It is entirely legitimate. It is not tax evasion, and it does not involve anything aggressive or complicated.
This service handles both. A properly prepared return that keeps you compliant and filed ahead of the deadline, with HMRC correspondence handled by a qualified professional — and a proactive review of your full financial picture that identifies every legal opportunity to reduce your liability before the tax year ends, not after it.
WHAT WE OFFER
Everything you need, filed and planned.
Review of All Income Sources
We carry out a thorough review of all your income for the tax year — employment income, rental income, freelance or self-employed earnings, dividends, savings interest, capital gains, and any other sources. Nothing is overlooked, and nothing is included that should not be.
Deductions, Reliefs & Allowances
